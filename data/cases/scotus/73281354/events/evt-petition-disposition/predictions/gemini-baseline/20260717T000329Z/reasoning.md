# Reasoning: AstraZeneca Pharmaceuticals LP v. Mosaic Health, Inc.

## The Case
This petition for certiorari arises from a Second Circuit antitrust decision regarding the application of the *Illinois Brick* indirect-purchaser rule to "lost profit" damages and the pleading standards for an antitrust conspiracy based on an "opportunity to conspire" under *Twombly*.

## The Record
The case is currently in a "forward" pending state. The petition was filed on March 5, 2026. Two high-profile amicus briefs (including one from the Chamber of Commerce and NAM) were filed at the petition stage on April 10, 2026. The petition was distributed for the May 14, 2026 conference. However, before the conference, the Court requested a response on April 30, 2026, and the time for response has been extended to July 22, 2026.

## Analysis
The baseline grant rate for paid discretionary cert petitions in recent Terms is approximately 2.5% to 3.0%. However, several strong positive signals are present here:
1. **Response Requested:** The Court proactively requested a response after the petition was distributed, indicating that at least one Justice found the petition sufficiently meritorious to warrant further briefing. This significantly elevates the probability of a grant over the base rate.
2. **Amicus Support:** The presence of prominent business groups (Chamber of Commerce, NAM, EPSA) filing amicus briefs at the certiorari stage is a strong indicator of the case's salience and legal importance.
3. **Subject Matter:** The petition presents two significant antitrust questions involving the boundaries of *Illinois Brick* and the pleading standards under *Twombly*, areas where the Supreme Court has historically shown interest in maintaining uniformity.

Despite these strong signals, the absolute probability of a grant remains below 50%. The Supreme Court denies the vast majority of petitions even when a response is requested and amicus support is present. The Second Circuit origin has a slightly higher base grant rate (~5.4%), but without further indicators such as multiple relists or a CVSG at this stage, the most probable outcome is still a denial.

## Conclusion
Given the strong positive signals (amicus support and a requested response), the probability of a grant is elevated significantly above the baseline to approximately 18%. However, because the probability remains below 50%, the predicted binary outcome is a denial.

**Probability of Grant:** 0.18
**Predicted Disposition:** Denied
