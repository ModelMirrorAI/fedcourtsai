## Questions and governing considerations

The petition presents two federal antitrust questions. First, it asks whether *Illinois Brick*'s direct-purchaser rule bars an indirect purchaser's lost-profit claim based on transactions it did not make. Second, it asks whether an opportunity to conspire through common lobbying and trade-association participation can help make parallel conduct plausibly conspiratorial under *Twombly*. At cert, the principal considerations are a square and durable circuit conflict, importance, clean presentation, and whether this interlocutory vehicle is suitable—not which side should ultimately prevail.

The provisioned questions-presented text and 39-page petition identify a substantial first-question split. The Second Circuit allowed respondents' lost-access theory despite their purchases through wholesalers; the Third and Sixth Circuits treat *Illinois Brick* as plaintiff-specific rather than damages-specific. CourtListener material for the Sixth Circuit's January 2026 denial of rehearing in *Academy of Allergy & Asthma v. Amerigroup Tennessee* strengthens this signal: separate published statements expressly discuss the Second Circuit's *Mosaic* approach, recognize the disagreement, and identify possible Supreme Court review. The petition also alleges a broader split over whether lobbying or trade-association participation is a plus factor, although that issue may be fact-bound because the Second Circuit considered other alleged plus factors as well.
