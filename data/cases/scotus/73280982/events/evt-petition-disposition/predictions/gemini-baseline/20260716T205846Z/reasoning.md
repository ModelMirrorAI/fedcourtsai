# Prediction Reasoning: Robinhood Markets, Inc. v. Vinod Sodha

**Legal Question & Context:**
This case presents two questions regarding the disclosure obligations of public companies under Section 11(a) of the Securities Act of 1933 and Item 303 of Regulation S-K. Specifically, the petition asks whether an issuer must disclose all material interim financial data without an inquiry into whether the omission rendered an affirmative statement misleading, and whether failing to disclose and quantify interim financial data before quarterly reporting deadlines violates Item 303 (and thus Section 11(a)). The Ninth Circuit held that Robinhood was required to disclose this interim data regarding a downward trend in trading metrics ahead of its IPO.

This is a major issue for securities law and public companies, as it threatens to impose continuous disclosure obligations during the IPO process. The Supreme Court recently addressed a closely related issue in *Macquarie Infrastructure Corp. v. Moab Partners, L. P.* (2024), holding that pure omissions under Item 303 cannot support a private action under Section 10(b) and Rule 10b-5 unless the omission makes other statements misleading. The current petition extends this battlefield to Section 11(a).
