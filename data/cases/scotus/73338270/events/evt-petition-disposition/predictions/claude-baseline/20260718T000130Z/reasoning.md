# Boysen v. PeaceHealth, No. 25-1280 — cert petition disposition

**Prediction: denied. P(any grant, GVR included) = 0.04.**

## The case

Twelve former PeaceHealth healthcare workers were terminated in 2021 after
refusing COVID-19 vaccination under Oregon's healthcare-worker mandate (Gov.
Brown / Oregon Health Authority). The district court (D. Or.) dismissed all
claims under Rule 12(b)(6); the Ninth Circuit affirmed in an **unpublished
memorandum** on December 3, 2025, holding the claims foreclosed by its
published decision in *Curtis v. Inslee*, 154 F.4th 678 (9th Cir. 2025) — a
case brought by other PeaceHealth employees against Washington's parallel
mandate.

The question presented asks whether the Due Process Clause prohibits a state
from conditioning the right to work in a licensed profession on accepting an
"investigational" (EUA) drug and waiving judicial remedies for resulting
injuries (via PREP Act immunity, 42 U.S.C. § 247d-6d). The petition's
principal arguments are (1) a party-presentation violation (*Sineneng-Smith*)
in the panel's sua sponte reliance on *Curtis*; (2) misapplication of
*Jacobson v. Massachusetts*; (3) FDCA/PREP-Act preemption and an
unconstitutional-conditions theory resting on *Roth*, *Perry*, and *Frost
Trucking*; and (4) a request for consolidated review with four companion
petitions.

## The decisive retrieval finding: the companion cluster is dead

The petition itself identifies four pending companion petitions and asks for
consolidated review. Forward-mode retrieval (public order lists, both
verified directly against the supremecourt.gov PDFs) shows all four were
denied in the weeks before and after this petition's first conference:

- *Health Freedom Defense Fund v. Carvalho*, No. 25-765 (the highest-profile
  vehicle — en banc Ninth Circuit, state-AG and NCLA amicus support):
  **denied May 18, 2026**, no recorded dissent.
- *Curtis v. Inslee*, No. 25-1119 (the published decision this case rests
  on, same employer PeaceHealth): **denied June 1, 2026**, no recorded
  dissent.
- *Sweeney v. Univ. of Colorado Hospital Authority*, No. 25-1055: **denied
  June 1, 2026**.
- *Horsley v. Kaiser Foundation*, No. 25-1203: **denied June 1, 2026**.

These denials predate the snapshot and are legitimate forward signal (flagged
per the contract). They show the Court passed on this exact issue — including
the published *Curtis* decision whose review this petition principally seeks
— without a single noted dissent. With *Curtis* denied, the "consolidated
review" theory is gone, and a plenary grant in the weakest vehicle of the
cluster (unpublished memorandum, sua sponte-affirmance posture) would be
extraordinary. A GVR is essentially unavailable: there is no intervening
grant to GVR in light of.

## Calibration

~2.5–3.0% (Term 2025: 2.5%); CA9-originating petitions 3.0%; petitions never
relisted 0.8%. This is a paid petition with a CFR — a cut the statpack does

(someone in chambers still wants the BIO — perhaps for the PREP-Act
remedy-waiver angle the companions lacked, or the party-presentation issue),
which keeps this above the no-signal baseline; but the cluster-wide,
dissent-less denials are much stronger evidence of no appetite for the
underlying question. I land at **P(grant) = 0.04** — above the ~2.5% blind
baseline out of respect for the CFR, well below the naive CFR rate. Most
likely path: BIO filed August 31, distribution in the late-September 2026
long conference, denial in early OT2026.
