# Mohan v. Watkins, No. 25-952 — petition disposition

## The case

The Solicitor General (D. John Sauer, counsel of record) petitions on behalf
of Dr. Brij Mohan and the United States from a divided Seventh Circuit panel
(*Watkins v. Mohan*, No. 24-1151, 144 F.4th 926, decided July 16, 2025;
rehearing denied November 3, 2025). Jordan Watkins, then a pretrial detainee
at the MCC Chicago, alleged deliberate indifference to severe post-surgical
pain and swelling after hernia repair; the panel majority held his claim
"fit[s] squarely within the *Bivens* claim recognized by *Carlson v. Green*,"
446 U.S. 14 (1980). Judge Kirsch dissented in relevant part, arguing the case
presents a new *Bivens* context (chronic/non-emergent injury, pretrial
detention facility, post-PLRA statutory landscape, BOP Administrative Remedy
Program).

## The question and the governing standard

The QP is whether *Carlson* permits a pretrial detainee's Fifth/Eighth
Amendment deliberate-indifference claim for chronic post-surgical pain.
Under *Ziglar v. Abbasi*, *Egbert v. Boule*, and *Goldey v. Fields* (2025,
summary reversal), a *Bivens* claim fails if it arises in a new context and
any special factor counsels hesitation. The Court has rejected every proposed
*Bivens* extension for 45 years. The petition describes a circuit conflict:
CA11 (*Johnson v. Terry*) effectively forecloses *Carlson* claims; CA3
(*Muniz*) nearly so; CA1/CA10 limit *Carlson* to its facts; CA7 and CA9 apply
it broadly.

## The decisive structural fact

The petition's **lead request is not a plenary grant**: the SG asks the Court
to grant the earlier-filed *Nielsen v. Watanabe*, No. 25-417 (CA9, same
question with a convicted prisoner — the cleaner vehicle) **and hold this
petition**, granting this one only in the alternative (Pet. 23-25).

Retrieval (forward cell, so unrestricted; see `retrieval.md`) shows the Court
did exactly that: **cert was granted in *Nielsen* on June 22, 2026**, for
argument in OT2026, out of the same June 18, 2026 conference to which this
petition was distributed. This petition's docket (snapshot of 2026-07-16)
shows distributions for the June 11 and June 18 conferences and **no order
since** — it was neither granted nor denied at those conferences or on the
end-of-term order lists. That is the classic hold pattern: 25-952 is being
held for *Nielsen*.

## From hold to disposition

A held companion's disposition is derivative of the lead case's merits
outcome, expected by ~June 2027:

- **Government wins *Nielsen*** (CA9 reversed; *Carlson* confined or held
  moribund): the Seventh Circuit's judgment for Watkins — which rests on
  reading *Carlson* broadly — cannot stand as-is, so the standard treatment
  is **GVR** "for further consideration in light of *Nielsen*." Estimated
  P(government wins) ≈ 0.88: the Court has ruled against every *Bivens*
  plaintiff since 1980, summarily reversed a *Carlson* extension just last
  Term in *Goldey*, and grants in this line overwhelmingly to reverse.
  P(GVR | government win) ≈ 0.95 (a plain denial after a government win is
  implausible; a plenary grant to address the pretrial-detainee wrinkle is
  possible but rare, and would still count as a grant).
- **Watkins-side outcome in *Nielsen*** (CA9 affirmed, *Carlson* reaffirmed
  as applied): the Seventh Circuit's judgment is consistent with it → this
  petition is **denied**. P ≈ 0.08.
- **DIG or other non-decision in *Nielsen***: P ≈ 0.04; this petition then
  becomes a candidate replacement vehicle (partly grant-side).

Aggregate P(grant, GVR included) ≈ 0.88 × 0.95 + ~0.02 ≈ **0.85**, with
**gvr** as the single likeliest disposition label. Residual mass: denied
(~0.12), dismissed/withdrawn (~0.02, e.g., settlement — district-court
proceedings are active, answer was due June 30, 2026).
