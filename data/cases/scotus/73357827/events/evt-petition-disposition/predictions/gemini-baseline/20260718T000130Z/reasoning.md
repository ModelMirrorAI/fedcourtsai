## Legal Question and Context
This case presents a cert petition regarding whether the Help America Vote Act (HAVA) creates federal rights enforceable by private litigants through 42 U.S.C. § 1983, and whether the total deprivation of HAVA-mandated administrative procedures constitutes a concrete Article III injury-in-fact. The petition alleges a deep circuit split on this issue.

## Posture and Predictors
The petition was filed on May 11, 2026. On June 11, the respondents (Don Mills, et al., represented by the Wisconsin Department of Justice) filed a waiver of right to respond. An amicus brief was also filed supporting the petition. The case was distributed for the September 28, 2026 Conference.

## Reasoning
The waiver of the right to respond is a very strong negative signal for a cert petition. The Court rarely grants a petition without first hearing from the respondent; typically, if any justice is interested in granting, the Court will issue a Call For Response (CFR) and relist the case. Currently, the case has not been relisted (relist count: 0), and is distributed for its first conference without a response. Base rates for modern cert petitions with 0 relists are overwhelmingly "denied" (around 97.3%). While the presence of an amicus brief and a claimed circuit split on a statutory civil rights issue raise the salience slightly, the most likely outcome at this specific procedural juncture is a denial. If the Court issues a CFR, the probability would shift, but predicting the ultimate disposition based on the current record strongly points to a denial. The probability of a grant is placed around 0.8%, reflecting the overall base rate for 0-relist petitions.

## Conclusion
Given the procedural posture (waiver of response, 0 relists), the petition is highly likely to be denied. I assign a grant probability of 0.8% and predict a disposition of `denied`.
