# Prediction

I predict denial, with a 44% probability of a grant-side disposition. If the Court grants relief, the overwhelmingly likely form is a summary grant, vacatur, and remand under *United States v. Munsingwear*, not plenary review of the habeas or Suspension Clause issues.

## Question and governing standard

The petition asks only whether the Court should vacate the Fourth Circuit's judgment and remand with instructions to dismiss after Bell's challenged sentence became moot. Under *Munsingwear* and *U.S. Bancorp Mortgage Co. v. Bonner Mall Partnership*, vacatur is an equitable remedy ordinarily favored when mootness prevents review through circumstances not attributable to the party seeking relief. It is less favored when that party caused the mootness by voluntary action.

## Case-specific signals

The provisioned March 27 petition and its questions presented establish a strong threshold case for mootness: Bell completed supervised release on December 24, 2025, after the Fourth Circuit issued its mandate, and he challenges only an expired sentence. The petition also says he sought a stay of the mandate before his supervision ended, supporting diligence. Vacatur would prevent the unreviewed Fourth Circuit holding from binding future prisoners on whether *Jones v. Hendrix* forecloses Section 2241 constitutional claims without creating a Suspension Clause problem.

The docket snapshot contains an unusually meaningful positive signal. The respondent initially waived a response, but the Court requested one on April 22. That moves the petition well above the ordinary cert-petition base rate and is consistent with the Court considering summary vacatur. It is a paid petition with experienced counsel, and the narrow requested remedy avoids the need to resolve the difficult underlying constitutional question.

The principal negative is the equitable causation issue. The petition itself says Bell moved for early termination of supervision and obtained a reduction to eighteen months. That voluntary motion accelerated the event that mooted the case, giving the government a substantial argument under *Bancorp* that this is not pure happenstance. The petition counters that the sentencing court independently set the term, the government supported termination, and Bell had already overserved by nearly seven years, but the issue prevents treating vacatur as routine. The government response is not yet provisioned; it is due July 22, so the Court lacks the most informative indication of whether the respondent agrees to vacatur. There is also no relist or CVSG signal in the snapshot.

The committed statpack reports a 2.5% grant rate for estimated resolved 2025 Term cert petitions and a 4.6% grant rate for modern discretionary petitions from the Fourth Circuit. Those broad priors understate this selected petition because a response was requested and the relief sought is a procedural GVR, but they counsel against making grant the modal outcome before the government's position is known. Balancing the clean mootness showing and response request against voluntary-causation uncertainty, I assign 44% to any grant and make denial the narrow modal call.
