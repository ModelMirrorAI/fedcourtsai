# Reasoning

## The Legal Question
The petitioner, Miguel Angel Delgado, Jr., challenges his conviction under 18 U.S.C. § 242 (deprivation of rights under color of law) and 18 U.S.C. § 1519 (falsification of records). The petition raises questions regarding the Fourth Amendment totality-of-the-circumstances framework (whether analysis can be temporally confined to the moment of force), the mens rea "willfulness" standard under § 242 (whether objective unreasonableness suffices), the definition of "bodily injury" under § 242, and the dependency of the § 1519 conviction on the § 242 conviction.

## The Posture and Baseline
This is a paid petition seeking review of a criminal conviction affirmed by the Fifth Circuit. The United States, as respondent, waived its right to respond on June 30, 2026, and the petition has been distributed for the September 28, 2026 conference without a response requested or a relist.

## Analysis and Prediction
A waiver by the Solicitor General in a criminal case strongly signals the government's view that the petition lacks merit, presents no deep circuit split, or suffers from vehicle problems, and thus does not warrant the Court's review. The Supreme Court grants certiorari in cases where the respondent waives only in exceedingly rare circumstances, and typically only after first requesting a response.

The questions presented are highly fact-bound, focusing on the specific application of the Fourth Amendment excessive force framework and the evidentiary basis for finding "willfulness" and "bodily injury" in this particular trial. While the petition attempts to frame the issue broadly (invoking the Court's rejection of temporally constrained analysis in precedent), it ultimately appears to be a challenge to the sufficiency of the evidence and the jury instructions in a specific § 242 prosecution. 

The base rate for a modern discretionary-cert petition with 0 relists is approximately 0.8%. Given the highly fact-bound nature of the petition and the Solicitor General's waiver of response, the probability of a grant is even lower than the baseline. I predict the petition will be denied.
