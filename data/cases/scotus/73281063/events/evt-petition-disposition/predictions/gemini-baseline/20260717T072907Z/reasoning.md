# Reasoning

**Legal Question and Posture**
This case involves a challenge to two Arizona voting laws enacted in 2022. The district court, after a trial, found no discriminatory purpose under *Village of Arlington Heights*. A Ninth Circuit panel reversed, finding organizational standing and holding that the totality of the circumstances suggested intentional discrimination. Judge Bumatay dissented, and 11 judges dissented from the denial of rehearing en banc. Arizona seeks certiorari on two questions: (1) whether Article III allows organizational standing when an unknown number of unidentified members "may be" injured, and (2) whether the Ninth Circuit improperly reweighed evidence of discriminatory purpose on clear error review.

**Signals and Context**
1. **High Salience:** The case involves state election integrity laws in a swing state, an issue of intense interest to the current Supreme Court majority.
2. **Lower Court Division:** An 11-judge dissent from denial of rehearing en banc in the Ninth Circuit is a very strong signal for Supreme Court review, particularly when conservative judges highlight an alleged departure from established standards (clear error review) by a liberal panel.
3. **Solicitor General's Position:** The U.S. Solicitor General filed a Brief in Opposition covering this petition (25-1022) and two companion petitions (25-1017, 25-1019). While the SG recommended a grant in 25-1017 (on an NVRA question), it recommended denying 25-1022, characterizing it as "fact-bound error correction." Notably, however, the SG conceded that the Ninth Circuit's decision on discriminatory intent was "plainly wrong." The Supreme Court frequently ignores the SG's recommendation to deny when the SG admits the lower court erred and the issue is highly salient.
4. **Docket Activity:** The petition was distributed for the June 18, 2026 conference. A reply was filed on June 3. It was then redistributed for the June 25, 2026 conference. This constitutes at least one relist, which historically elevates the grant probability significantly (from ~3% to ~8% overall, and much higher for targeted, high-profile cases).

**Conclusion**
Given the 11-judge en banc dissent, the substantive issue (election law and discriminatory intent findings), the SG's concession of lower court error, and the relist, the Court is highly likely to grant certiorari to correct the Ninth Circuit. While the SG frames QP2 as fact-bound, the Court often takes such cases to discipline circuit courts on the proper application of clear error review (especially concerning *Arlington Heights* and voting laws). 

**Big Case Score:** 0.85 (High-stakes election law dispute with national implications).
