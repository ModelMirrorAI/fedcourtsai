## Legal Question and Posture
The petition asks whether the Bankruptcy Code's safe harbor for securities settlement payments, 11 U.S.C. § 546(e), applies extraterritorially to prevent foreign liquidators from pursuing foreign statutory and common-law avoidance claims that would otherwise be cognizable in foreign courts. Petitioners, foreign liquidators for the Fairfield Sentry funds (feeder funds to the Bernard L. Madoff investment scheme), appeal a Second Circuit decision holding that § 546(e) shields these transactions and extinguishes the foreign claims. The petition alleges that the Second Circuit's ruling breaks with other circuits (such as the Fifth Circuit's decision in *Condor*) and violates principles of international comity and the presumption against extraterritoriality underlying Chapter 15 of the Bankruptcy Code. 

## Case Record and Salience
The petition arises from the massive, multi-jurisdictional unwinding of the Madoff Ponzi scheme, an issue of substantial commercial importance. Two amicus briefs were filed at the petition stage (by Keith Lloyd et al. and Leif Clark et al.), suggesting elevated interest among bankruptcy practitioners and scholars. However, the brief in opposition argues that the claimed circuit split is illusory, noting that the Fifth Circuit's *Condor* decision dealt with 11 U.S.C. § 1521(a)(7) and did not specifically analyze the extraterritorial reach of § 546(e).
