# Elkins v. United States, No. 25-1061 — cert prediction

**Prediction: deny. P(any grant, GVR included) = 0.07.**

## The case

Holly Ann Elkins was convicted of conspiracy to stalk (18 U.S.C. § 371),
cyberstalking resulting in death (§ 2261A(2), § 2261(b)), and a § 924(c)
firearm count, arising from a stalking campaign against her fiancé's
ex-girlfriend that culminated in the fiancé murdering the victim. The Fifth
Circuit (Richman, J.) vacated the § 924(c) count (cyberstalking under
§ 2261A(2)(B) is not categorically a crime of violence) but affirmed the
cyberstalking and conspiracy convictions, rejecting her as-applied Commerce
Clause challenge on circuit precedent (*Marek*): "the use of a phone — even
intrastate — involves the use of an instrumentality of interstate commerce."
161 F.4th 899, 912 (5th Cir. 2025). She has a life sentence on the affirmed
cyberstalking count.

The question presented: whether the Fifth Circuit's *categorical* approach to
Lopez category two — phones just *are* instrumentalities of interstate
commerce — is constitutional, in claimed conflict with the Tenth Circuit's
*United States v. Chavarria*, 140 F.4th 1257 (10th Cir. 2025) (motor vehicles
are not per se instrumentalities under the federal kidnapping statute).

## Signals pointing toward a grant

- **Call for response.** The United States waived (Mar 19, 2026); the petition
  was distributed for the April 17 conference; and on April 13 the Court
  requested a response. A CFR means at least one chambers took the QP
  seriously — the single strongest positive signal on this docket.
- **Paid, counseled petition** with a clean QP and a petition-stage amicus
  (Texas Criminal Defense Lawyers Association).
- **Live doctrinal ferment.** *Chavarria* is recent (2025); Judge Calvert's
  partial dissent in *Bryan* (11th Cir. 2025) calls it a "developing circuit
  split"; Judge Murphy's *Allen* concurrence (6th Cir.) voices the same
  concern. Justices Thomas and Gorsuch have shown appetite for policing
  Commerce Clause limits.
- **Stakes.** Petitioner will die in prison if the affirmance stands; the QP
  is preserved, reviewed de novo below, and outcome-determinative on the
  court of appeals' own reasoning.

## Signals pointing toward a denial

- **The split is not square, and the BIO exploits that.** *Chavarria*
  concerned "motor vehicles" — a generic class the Tenth Circuit said is not
  *per se* an instrumentality — while the Tenth Circuit itself holds that
  *telephones are* instrumentalities of interstate commerce (*Morgan*, 748
  F.3d 1024, 1033 n.11 (10th Cir. 2014)). *Chavarria* expressly preserved
  per se treatment for telegraph-like items. So on phones — the only item at
  issue here — there is no circuit on Elkins's side; the SG's "no conflict"
  argument is essentially correct, and every circuit to decide the phone
  question (1st, 2d, 5th, 6th, 8th, 9th, 11th) has gone the government's way.
- **Vehicle problems.** The indictment and trial evidence involved a computer,
  the internet, email, a GPS tracker (transmitting through satellites and
  servers "in the U.S. and throughout the world"), and a phone. Even if the
  Court rejected per se treatment of phones, multiple alternative
  instrumentalities — some with actual interstate transmission — would
  support the conviction, so the QP may not be outcome-dispositive.
- **Ugly facts.** The district court found petitioner the "mastermind" of a
  campaign of terror ending in the victim's murder — unattractive facts for a
  federalism landmark.
- **Recent denials on the same terrain.** The Court denied cert in *Smith*
  (146 S. Ct. 356 (2025)), *Stackhouse* (145 S. Ct. 558 (2024)), and
  *Uhlenbrock* (145 S. Ct. 2853 (2025)) — repeated recent passes on
  phone/instrumentality Commerce Clause challenges.
- **No companion vehicle.** A CourtListener docket search shows no pending
  SCOTUS petition in *Chavarria* or *Bryan*, so there is no better-postured
  case the Court is holding this one for — but also no pressure to resolve
  the issue now; the "developing" split can percolate until a circuit
  actually holds phones non-categorical.
