# Reasoning: Holly Ann Elkins v. United States

## The Legal Question
The core issue is whether the Fifth Circuit's categorical approach to "instrumentalities of interstate commerce" is constitutional under the Commerce Clause, specifically concerning mobile phones. The Fifth Circuit (*United States v. Elkins*, 2025) held that phones are categorically instrumentalities of interstate commerce, whereas the Tenth Circuit (*United States v. Chavarria*, 2025) rejected a categorical approach, insisting that an object's use must actually affect interstate commerce to warrant federal interest.

## Governing Standard & Base Rates
Under *United States v. Lopez*, Congress may regulate instrumentalities of interstate commerce, or persons/things in interstate commerce, even if the threat comes only from intrastate activities. The interpretation of this category creates a clear doctrinal split.
The general modern discretionary-cert grant base rate is low (~3.2%). For the Fifth Circuit, the rate is roughly 4.8%.
