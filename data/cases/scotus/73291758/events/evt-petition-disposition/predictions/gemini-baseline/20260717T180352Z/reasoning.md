# Reasoning for Prediction

## The Case
The petitioners, Ronald and Katie Dittmer (pro se), seek review of a decision by the Appellate Court of Illinois, First District (Nos. 1-24-1269, 1-24-1274), which seemingly upheld domestic violence orders of protection entered against them. They raise a series of constitutional challenges (Free Exercise, Free Speech, Second Amendment, Due Process, Equal Protection) to the Illinois Domestic Violence Act (750 ILCS 60). They also complain of "ineffective assistance of counsel", "plain error", and "procedural irregularities".

## Standard for Certiorari
Certiorari is rarely granted, and typically only when there is a significant circuit split on an important question of federal law, or a state court of last resort has decided an important federal question in a way that conflicts with the decision of another state court of last resort or of a United States court of appeals. (Supreme Court Rule 10). "Error correction" is not a valid basis for a cert grant.

## Application to Facts
The questions presented essentially ask the Supreme Court to review the specific protective orders entered against them by a state court and argue that their former counsel was ineffective. This is a classic "error correction" petition. 

Furthermore, the petition originates from a state intermediate appellate court, not a state court of last resort, and it is a pro se filing (though listed as "paid"). There is no indication of a split in authority, and the petition relies almost entirely on alleged factual and procedural errors in the lower courts.

According to the pipeline's historical context on modern discretionary-cert petitions, petitions with 0 relists, no CVSG, and originating from a state court with these characteristics have an extremely low base rate of being granted (roughly 1% overall, and effectively near zero for pro se error-correction cases).

## Conclusion
The petition is overwhelmingly likely to be denied.
