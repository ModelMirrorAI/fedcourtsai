## Analysis

**Legal Question & Governing Standard:**
The petition presents the question of whether Washington state's ban on "large capacity magazines" (capable of holding more than 10 rounds), enacted in ESSB 5078, violates the Second Amendment. The governing standard is the Supreme Court's historical tradition test articulated in *NYSRPA v. Bruen* and *DC v. Heller*. 

**Facts & Record:**
The Washington Supreme Court upheld the ban, finding that LCMs are not "Arms" under the Second Amendment or, alternatively, that the ban is consistent with the nation's historical tradition of firearm regulation. The petitioners filed for certiorari in August 2025 (Docket 25-153). 

The case was distributed for conference over 20 times between September 2025 and June 2026. Supplemental briefs were filed in early 2026 notifying the Court of the D.C. Court of Appeals panel decision in *Benson v. United States* (striking down a similar ban) and its subsequent vacatur for rehearing en banc, highlighting the lower court volatility and potential circuit splits regarding magazine bans. 

**Reasoning:**
The Supreme Court has taken a keen interest in lower court application of *Bruen* to bans on assault weapons and large capacity magazines. The sheer number of relists for this petition—matching the treatment of other high-profile magazine and assault weapon ban petitions like *Duncan v. Bonta*—strongly indicates that the Court was holding the case pending a vehicle to address the issue. 

On July 1, 2026 (prior to this snapshot), the Court granted certiorari in two major assault weapon ban cases, *Viramontes v. Cook County* and *Grant v. Higgins*, while carrying over magazine ban cases like *Duncan* to the October 2026 term. This strongly implies that *Gator's Custom Guns* is being held pending the resolution of *Viramontes* and *Grant*. 

Because the Supreme Court's conservative supermajority is expected to clarify the *Bruen* standard in a way that is favorable to Second Amendment challengers in the granted cases, the standard operating procedure will be to issue a Grant, Vacate, and Remand (GVR) for the held cases. Therefore, rather than a plenary grant or a denial, the most likely ultimate disposition for this petition is a GVR directing the Washington Supreme Court to reconsider the case in light of the forthcoming decisions in *Viramontes* and *Grant*.

**Confidence:**
I am highly confident (85%) in a GVR outcome. The relist pattern and the recent grants in companion Second Amendment cases make a "hold for GVR" the standard procedural maneuver.
