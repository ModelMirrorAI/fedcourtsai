# NAGR v. Lamont, No. 25-421 — petition disposition (forward mode)

## The case

Petitioners (National Association for Gun Rights and an individual member)
challenge Connecticut's assault-weapons and large-capacity-magazine bans. The
district court denied a preliminary injunction (D. Conn., Aug. 3, 2023) and the
Second Circuit affirmed on August 22, 2025 in a consolidated decision that also
resolved the companion appeal *Grant v. Lamont* (Nos. 23-1162, 23-1344). The
question presented: whether a ban on AR-15-style rifles and >10-round magazines
— arms "possessed by millions of law-abiding Americans for lawful purposes" —
violates the Second Amendment. The posture is **interlocutory** (appeal from a
PI denial), which the petition concedes and argues around; the BIO leans hard
on vehicle defects (preliminary undeveloped record, disputed facts, waiver of
two independent alternative grounds for affirmance).

## The docket signal

The snapshot shows an extraordinarily engaged petition: paid case, full
briefing (BIO after two contested extensions, reply, dueling supplemental
briefs in March 2026, a respondents' letter re *Benson* in April 2026), and
**distribution for roughly fifteen consecutive conferences** from 1/23/2026
through the 6/29/2026 end-of-term clean-up conference — with no order entered.
The Court carried the petition over the summer rather than resolving it at the
clean-up conference.

## Analysis

The Court has chosen its merits vehicles and this petition was not one of them
— unsurprising given its PI posture and the waiver arguments in the BIO, when
the same Second Circuit judgment was available in *Grant* on a cleaner
footing. From here, 25-421 is a classic **held companion**: it raises the
identical question as the granted cases (and arises from the same judgment as
*Grant*), so the near-certain path is a hold pending the merits decision,
followed by:

- **GVR** in light of *Grant/Viramontes* if the Court vacates or reverses the
  Second Circuit — the modal outcome. The Court granted the petitions
  challenging ban-*upholding* judgments (rather than taking *Benson* to review
  a ban-striking one), after Justice Kavanaugh's *Snope* statement that the
  Court should address the AR-15 question "in the next Term or two" and that
  the common-use argument was "strong." Vehicle selection plus the Court's
  post-*Bruen* composition point toward reversal or at least vacatur-and-remand
  under a clarified standard. I put P(petitioner-favorable merits outcome in
  the granted cases) ≈ 0.80, and GVR is the standard disposition for a
  same-judgment companion when that happens (≈ 0.90 conditional).
- **Denial** if the Court affirms the Second Circuit (≈ 0.20 of merits paths),
  or in the small residual where the Court denies early at the long conference
  or GVR practice is bypassed.
- A later **plenary grant/consolidation** of 25-421 itself is unlikely (≈ 3%):
  the Court just passed over it in favor of the companions.

Base rates frame but do not drive this: the statpack's modern-cert anchor is a
~2.5–3% grant rate, rising to ~22% at 3+ relists (this case is far beyond that
bucket, and serial relisting historically resolves either to a grant-side
disposition or an opinion-accompanied denial — *Snope* itself was a
14-relist denial). Here the case-specific information — a granted companion
from the same judgment — dominates the priors.

## Probability

P(grant-side disposition, GVR included) ≈ **0.71**
(≈ 0.68 GVR + ≈ 0.03 plenary grant; denial ≈ 0.28; dismissal/other ≈ 0.01).
Predicted disposition: **gvr**, `granted = 1`. Timing: likely June–July 2027,
after the merits decision in the granted cases. Confidence 0.6 — the hold
mechanics are near-certain, so the residual uncertainty is almost entirely the
merits outcome in *Grant/Viramontes*.

## Big-case score

0.7 — the underlying question (constitutionality of state AR-15/LCM bans) is
one of the highest-stakes questions in current constitutional litigation, with
a fresh circuit split and fourteen states plus D.C. affected. But this specific
petition is no longer the vehicle: its own disposition will most likely be a
one-line GVR or denial in the shadow of *Grant/Viramontes*, which caps its
standalone newsworthiness.
