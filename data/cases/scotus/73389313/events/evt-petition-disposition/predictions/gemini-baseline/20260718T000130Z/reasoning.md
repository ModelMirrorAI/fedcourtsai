## Prediction
**Denied** (Probability: 0.1%)

## Reasoning

This is a petition for a writ of certiorari filed by Joan E. Farr, a *pro se* litigant. The respondents include Alexandra Grant (an artist), GrantLOVE LLC, Scott Sims, Ziffren Brittenham LLP, WME IMG, LLC, and various "Federal Parties" (which, based on district court records, include the Department of Defense, Department of Justice, CIA, and FBI). 

All respondents waived their right to respond. A waiver of response strongly indicates that the respondents consider the petition to lack merit and not warrant the resources required to oppose it, unless the Court specifically requests a response. It was distributed for the September 28, 2026 conference without any respondent filing a brief in opposition.

A review of the lower court records reveals that the underlying suit (W.D. Mo. 4:24-cv-00439) is a civil rights action filed against a mix of celebrities, their management/legal representatives, and federal intelligence and law enforcement agencies. Such *pro se* cases alleging broad conspiracies involving celebrities and government entities are routinely dismissed by lower courts as frivolous or failing to state a claim, and they do not present the kind of compelling legal questions (such as a circuit split or an important federal question of first impression) that satisfy the criteria for certiorari under Supreme Court Rule 10.

Given the *pro se* nature of the petition, the bizarre mix of celebrity and government defendants, and the unanimous waivers of response, the likelihood of this petition being granted is virtually zero. The Court will undoubtedly deny certiorari.
