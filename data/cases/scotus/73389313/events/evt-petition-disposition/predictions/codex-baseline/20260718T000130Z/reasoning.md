## Governing standard

Supreme Court Rule 10 makes certiorari discretionary and ordinarily requires a compelling reason, such as a developed conflict among courts of appeals, conflict with controlling Supreme Court precedent, or an important unresolved federal question. Error correction in an individual case, fact-bound disputes, and vehicle defects generally weigh against review. A grant ordinarily requires four votes. A GVR would require some identifiable intervening authority or other reason to vacate and remand; none appears in this record.
