## Legal Question and Posture
The case presents an Eighth Amendment question regarding whether state corrections leadership must implement a "reasonable" aggregate statewide response to a public health emergency (COVID-19), and whether such obligations were clearly established between March 2020 and May 2022 to overcome qualified immunity. The petitioner, the State of Oregon, filed a petition for a writ of certiorari following an adverse decision by the Ninth Circuit.

## Disposition Analysis
On June 30, 2026, the petitioner (Oregon, et al.) submitted a "Motion of Oregon, et al. to dismiss" their own petition. Under Supreme Court Rule 46.2, when a petitioner files a motion to dismiss their own proceeding, the adverse party has an opportunity to object only as to costs. Absent an objection that is not met by the moving party, the Clerk will enter an order of dismissal without further reference to the Court.

Given that the petitioner has actively moved to dismiss their own petition, the probability of the Court granting certiorari is zero. The petition will inevitably be dismissed pursuant to Rule 46.
