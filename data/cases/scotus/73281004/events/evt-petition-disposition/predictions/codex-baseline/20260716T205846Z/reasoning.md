## Question and governing law

The petition asks whether the Eighth Amendment permits an aggregate, statewide assessment of Oregon corrections officials' COVID-19 response across fourteen facilities and more than two years, and whether any such right was clearly established for qualified-immunity purposes. On the merits, the petition invokes the deliberate-indifference framework of *Farmer v. Brennan* and argues that the Ninth Circuit defined the right at too high a level of generality under the Supreme Court's qualified-immunity cases. It emphasizes that the district court found Oregon's initial response constitutionally adequate in June 2020, yet later allowed a jury to assess the response as a whole.

Had the petition remained contested, several facts would have supplied some grant-side signal. The Court requested a response after respondents initially waived one, the asserted rule could affect emergency administration across a state prison system, and the Court has sometimes corrected overly general qualified-immunity analysis. But the petition identifies no developed circuit split; the Ninth Circuit's decision is an unpublished memorandum; the record combines different officials, facilities, policies, and phases of the pandemic; and the case remains at an interlocutory qualified-immunity stage. Those vehicle and percolation weaknesses would have kept the conditional chance of plenary review modest.
