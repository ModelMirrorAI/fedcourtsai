## Questions and governing standard

The petition asks (1) whether *Rooker-Feldman* bars a 42 U.S.C. § 1983 claim alleging an injury caused by official conduct rather than by a state-court judgment, and (2) whether Rule 15(a)(2) permits denial of leave to amend as futile together with prefiling restrictions at the pleading stage without an opportunity to amend. The petition relies principally on *Exxon Mobil* and *Skinner* for a narrow conception of *Rooker-Feldman*, *Foman* for liberal amendment, and access-to-court cases for safeguards before filing restrictions.

Under Supreme Court Rule 10, the decisive forecast question is not simply whether those doctrines matter, but whether this record cleanly presents a recurring conflict or important federal question. A genuine, outcome-determinative circuit conflict, a precedential decision below, a clean vehicle, and developed adversarial briefing would favor review. Fact-bound error correction, alternative grounds, and vehicle defects strongly disfavor it.

## Record-specific assessment

The recovered petition describes a pro se civil-rights action arising from Ohio domestic-relations and related enforcement proceedings. It says the district court dismissed some claims under *Rooker-Feldman*, found others inadequately pleaded, denied amendment as futile, and imposed filing restrictions; the Sixth Circuit affirmed in a nonprecedential disposition and also addressed immunity and several procedural motions. Those overlapping jurisdictional, merits, immunity, amendment, and filing-restriction grounds make the case a poor vehicle for either broad question.

The petition asserts two circuit divisions, but its cited cases largely state context-specific applications of settled standards. It does not establish a square, acknowledged split on a single legal rule, nor does it show that resolving either question would change the judgment despite the alternative grounds. Combining *Rooker-Feldman*, Rule 12, Rule 15, immunity, and filing restrictions in one petition further reduces the chance of plenary review.

The procedural signals are especially unfavorable. Respondents waived a response; no response was requested before distribution. The petition identifies a related petition by the same petitioner that was denied on May 5, 2025. The present case comes from an unpublished/nonprecedential affirmance, is self-represented, and concerns intensely case-specific allegations about judicial assignment, recusal, custody-related proceedings, and litigation restrictions. There is no evident federal-government interest, amicus support, or developed merits record.
