# Prediction: Denied

## Legal Question and Posture
The petition presents a question under 42 U.S.C. § 1981 regarding whether an African American restaurant patron can recover for racial discrimination when she was singled out and subjected to disparate treatment (forcibly having a shawl placed on her to cover her clothing) but ultimately stayed in the restaurant to accompany her friend and was not entirely denied service. The Fifth Circuit affirmed the District Court's summary judgment in favor of the defendant, finding no actionable deprivation of the right to make or enforce a contract.

## Analysis
Several factors strongly point toward a denial of certiorari:

1. **Lack of a Response (Waiver):** The docket indicates the petition was distributed for the September 28, 2026 conference without any record of a Brief in Opposition being filed or a Call for Response (CFR) being issued. The respondent effectively waived their right to respond. The Supreme Court almost never grants a petition without first calling for a response from the opposing party.
2. **Fact-Bound Nature:** The issue turns heavily on the specific facts of the encounter—whether the treatment amounted to an actionable denial of the benefits of a contract under § 1981, given that the petitioner ultimately remained and was served. This does not appear to implicate a deep, mature circuit split requiring the Court's immediate intervention.
3. **Base Rates:** The overall grant rate for modern discretionary-cert petitions is between 2.5% and 3.3%. For petitions from the Fifth Circuit, it is approximately 4.8%. However, for petitions with 0 relists, the grant rate drops precipitously to 0.8%. Without a relist, a CVSG, or even a response from the opposing party, the probability of a grant is extremely low.

## Conclusion
Given the fact-bound nature of the § 1981 claim, the absence of a brief in opposition or a call for response, and the extremely low base rate for such petitions, the petition is overwhelmingly likely to be denied. I assign a probability of 0.8% to a grant, consistent with the base rate for petitions with zero relists.
