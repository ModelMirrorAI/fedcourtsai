# Reasoning for evt-petition-disposition in scotus/73317900

This is a prediction for the disposition of the certiorari petition in *Burford German Funding LLC, et al. v. financialright claims GmbH*.

## Summary
The Supreme Court is highly likely to grant the petition, vacate the Third Circuit's judgment, and remand (GVR) for reconsideration in light of its recent decision in *Jules v. Andre Balazs Properties*, No. 25-83. 

## Analysis
The petition raises a question of federal jurisdiction under the Federal Arbitration Act (FAA): whether a federal court that has subject-matter jurisdiction over a pending matter also has subject-matter jurisdiction to resolve motions to stay and compel arbitration under FAA §§ 3 and 4. The Third Circuit held that it did not have jurisdiction to independently assess the FAA motions, purportedly relying on the Supreme Court's decision in *Badgerow v. Walters*, 596 U.S. 1 (2022). 

On May 14, 2026, the Supreme Court decided *Jules v. Andre Balazs Properties*, 608 U.S. __ (2026), addressing a very similar question. The Court in *Jules* held that a federal court with pre-existing jurisdiction over claims stayed under § 3 of the FAA *does* have jurisdiction to resolve motions to confirm or vacate an arbitral award (§§ 9 and 10), without needing an independent jurisdictional basis on the face of the motion. The Court reasoned that the original federal-question jurisdiction authorizing the court to adjudicate the claims also authorizes it to resolve FAA motions relating to those claims. This reasoning directly contradicts the Third Circuit's holding in this case.

The procedural timeline of this petition strongly signals a GVR:
1. **May 4, 2026**: Petition filed, explicitly requesting the Court to hold the petition for *Jules* and issue a GVR if *Jules* rejected the *Badgerow*-based jurisdictional theory.
2. **May 14, 2026**: Supreme Court issues its decision in *Jules*.
3. **June 9, 2026**: Respondent files a waiver of its right to respond.
4. **June 16, 2026**: Petitioners file a supplemental brief, likely to alert the Court that *Jules* has been decided and supports a GVR.
5. **July 15, 2026**: The Court requests a response.

Under Supreme Court practice, the Court will not grant or GVR a paid petition when the respondent has waived their right to respond without first calling for a response (CFR). The July 15 CFR, following the respondent's waiver and the intervening *Jules* decision, is the standard procedural prerequisite to a GVR. Given that *Jules* resolved the exact jurisdictional theory underpinning the Third Circuit's decision, the probability of a GVR is exceptionally high (0.95).
