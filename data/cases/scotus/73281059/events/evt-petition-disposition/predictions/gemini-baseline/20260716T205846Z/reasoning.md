# Prediction: Granted

**Probability: 95%**

## The Legal Question
The petition presents questions concerning whether the National Voter Registration Act (NVRA) preempts Arizona's laws that require documentary proof of citizenship (DPOC) to vote in federal elections using state-specific forms, and whether the NVRA preempts Arizona's restrictions on mail-in voting for those who have not provided DPOC. It also raises a question about discriminatory animus regarding H.B. 2243.

## The Governing Standard
The Supreme Court grants certiorari for compelling reasons, typically when a United States court of appeals has decided an important federal question in a way that conflicts with relevant decisions of the Supreme Court or when the case involves an important question of federal law that has not been, but should be, settled by the Court.

## Analysis
The likelihood of a grant in this case is exceptionally high, deviating significantly from the low overall base rate for cert petitions (~3%). 

1. **Prior Supreme Court Intervention**: The Supreme Court previously granted an emergency stay of the district court's injunction against the proof of citizenship requirement. A stay on the emergency docket requires the applicant to show a "fair prospect" that the Court will grant certiorari and a "likelihood" that the Court will reverse the judgment below. Cases where the Court has already intervened at the stay stage are overwhelmingly likely to be granted full merits review.
2. **Divided Lower Court**: The Ninth Circuit panel affirmed the injunction, and the circuit subsequently denied rehearing en banc over 11 dissents. A significant number of dissents from the denial of en banc review is a classic strong signal for Supreme Court review, particularly on ideological or highly salient issues.
3. **Salience and Importance**: This is a major election law case involving state sovereignty, federalism, and the reach of the NVRA regarding citizenship requirements for voting. These are issues of high priority and intense interest for the current Court. 

Given the prior stay, the 11 en banc dissents, and the fundamental federalism questions regarding election administration, the Court is nearly certain to grant the petition.

## Predicted Votes
We predict a grant (disposition: granted).
