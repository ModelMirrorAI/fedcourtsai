# Birks v. Aguirre-Jarquin, No. 25-1341 — cert petition disposition

**Prediction: denied. P(any grant, GVR included) = 0.05.**

## The case

Donna Birks, a Seminole County (FL) latent-print examiner, petitions from the
Eleventh Circuit's published decision in *Aguirre v. Seminole County*, 158
F.4th 1276 (11th Cir. 2025) (rehearing en banc denied Jan. 22, 2026), which
affirmed the denial of qualified immunity on a § 1983 Fourteenth Amendment
fabrication-of-evidence claim. Respondent Clemente Aguirre-Jarquin was
convicted of a 2004 double murder and sentenced to death, partly on Birks's
palm-print match to the murder weapon; outside examiners later declined to
confirm the match, the Florida Supreme Court vacated his conviction in 2016 on
other grounds (post-trial confessions by the victims' relative plus DNA), the
State dropped the retrial, and Aguirre sued for his 14 years of incarceration.

The five questions presented (from the provisioned QP text and petition) argue:
(1) causation/materiality is an essential element of a fabrication claim, and
Aguirre's own trial admission that he handled the knife breaks causation;
(2) a Fourteenth Amendment fabrication claim requires *knowing and
intentional* submission of false evidence, not the unknowing submission of
evidence later determined inaccurate; and (3–5) that it was not clearly
established in 2004–2006 that relying on a certified colleague to verify a
print match — while privately doubting his competence — violated due process,
so qualified immunity was wrongly denied.

## Case-specific adjustment

**Upward pressure — the *Zorn* path.** The realistic grant route here is not
plenary review but a QI summary reversal (or a grant limited to the
clearly-established question). In *Zorn v. Linton*, 607 U.S. ___ (Mar. 23,
2026) (per curiam) — which I retrieved and read, and which the petition itself
cites — the Court summarily reversed a Second Circuit QI denial 6-3 for
defining the right at too high a level of generality, over a Sotomayor dissent
protesting the "resurgence" of the one-way summary-reversal practice. Birks's
QPs 3–5 track *Zorn*/*Wesby*/*Emmons* almost verbatim, and the Eleventh
Circuit conceded its cases were not "directly on point" while relying on the
general principle that falsely attesting to incriminating evidence is
unlawful. A petition arguing exactly the analysis the Court just deployed, in
the same Term, from a published opinion, is a genuine summary-reversal
candidate. The mens-rea question (QP2) also has real inter-circuit texture —
most circuits require knowing fabrication (e.g., *Morphew*, 172 F.4th 802,
821 (10th Cir. 2026)), and the petition frames the Eleventh Circuit as having
let a jury treat a distrusted-verifier theory as the equivalent of fabrication.

**Downward pressure.** (a) The respondent **waived his response** (June 15,
2026), and no call for a response has issued; the Court essentially never
grants or summarily reverses without a response, so the grant path requires
CFR → BIO → redistribution → relists, each step with hazard. Respondent's
counsel (Grant & Eisenhofer) waiving signals confidence the petition reads as
fact-bound. (b) The petition *is* fact-bound: five sprawling,
fact-saturated QPs read as error correction, and QP1's causation theory asks
the Court to referee pendent appellate jurisdiction on an interlocutory QI
appeal. (c) Unlike *Zorn* (classic Fourth Amendment excessive force, the
Court's habitual summary-reversal territory), fabrication-of-evidence QI
denials are rarely summarily reversed, and on Aguirre's version of the facts —
a sham verification by an examiner she believed incapable, later unconfirmed
by every outside reviewer — the "knowing falsity" framing is contestable
rather than obviously absent. (d) The Eleventh Circuit already gave Birks
half a loaf (reversing on the Fourth Amendment malicious-prosecution claim),
softening the outlier posture. (e) The optics of summarily protecting the
analyst in one of Florida's most prominent death-row exonerations make this an
unattractive vehicle even for Justices sympathetic on QI doctrine.
