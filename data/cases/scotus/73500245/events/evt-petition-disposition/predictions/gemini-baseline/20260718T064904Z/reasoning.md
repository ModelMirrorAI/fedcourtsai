## Legal Context and Posture
This is a petition for a writ of certiorari filed by Jerry M. Blevins, a pro se attorney challenging a disciplinary action by the Alabama State Bar. The lower court decision comes from the Supreme Court of Alabama.

The Question Presented focuses on whether the Alabama Supreme Court's interpretation and application of § 34-3-62 of the Alabama Code violated Blevins's procedural due process rights under the Fourteenth Amendment.

## Case Specifics and Viability
The Supreme Court rarely grants review of state supreme court decisions regarding attorney discipline. These cases are overwhelmingly fact-bound and primarily involve the interpretation of state statutes or rules. To warrant Supreme Court review, a state case must present a clear, significant federal question—often one that has divided lower courts.

Here, the petition alleges a procedural due process violation. However, such claims in the context of state bar discipline are typically viewed as fact-specific grievances rather than vehicles for resolving important national legal issues. There is no indication of a split of authority regarding the underlying federal question.

Furthermore, the Alabama State Bar filed a waiver of its right to respond on July 1, 2026. This is a strong negative signal; respondents typically waive their right to respond when they believe a petition is patently meritless and highly unlikely to be granted, calculating that the Court will simply deny the petition without requesting a response.

## Procedural Status
The case was distributed for the September 28, 2026 conference. This is its first distribution. There are no relists, nor has the Court called for the views of the Solicitor General (CVSG) or requested a response from the respondent.

## Conclusion
Given the fact-bound nature of the case, its origin as a state bar disciplinary matter, the lack of a circuit split, the respondent's waiver, and the absence of any positive signals like relists, the probability of a grant is exceptionally low. The petition will almost certainly be denied.
