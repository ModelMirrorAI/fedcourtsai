# Reasoning

This is a pro se petition for a writ of certiorari arising from a federal habeas corpus proceeding in the Seventh Circuit. The petitioner claims constitutional violations regarding state jury instructions on self-defense and ineffective assistance of counsel.

- **Absence of a Response:** The respondent filed a waiver of the right to respond. The Supreme Court virtually never grants certiorari without first considering a response. While the Court might issue a Call for Response (CFR) if a Justice finds the petition interesting, the lack of a response heavily predicts an initial denial at conference.
- **Base Rates and Signals:** There are no relists and no CVSG. Base rates from `metrics/statpack.md` show that modern discretionary-cert petitions are overwhelmingly denied (~95%), with the Seventh Circuit having a historical grant rate of roughly 2.0%. 
- **Merits/Rule 10:** The Questions Presented focus on fact-bound, case-specific applications of established law (Boyde, Mullaney, Strickland) to a state criminal trial. The petition does not allege a circuit split on a pressing question of federal law, which is typically required for Supreme Court review under Rule 10.

Given the pro se nature, the fact-specific claims, the waiver of response, and the absence of any positive signals (like relists or amicus briefs), this petition is highly likely to be denied.
