# Pennsylvania v. Eakin, No. 25-967 — cert disposition prediction

**Prediction: P(any grant, GVR included) = 0.65; likeliest single disposition: granted (plenary).**

## The case

Pennsylvania's Election Code requires mail-in voters to handwrite a date next to
their signature on the ballot return envelope. A Third Circuit panel (Eakin v.
Adams County Bd. of Elections, 149 F.4th 291, Aug. 26, 2025) held, applying
Anderson-Burdick, that enforcing the date requirement by rejecting undated or
misdated ballots violates the First and Fourteenth Amendments. Rehearing en banc
was denied 7–6 over two dissents (Phipps, J.; Bove, J.). The Commonwealth,
through its Attorney General, petitions; the RNC-side respondents (appellants
below) support cert and filed their own companion petition (RNC v. Eakin,
No. 25-962, vided with this one). The Eakin/DSCC/DCCC respondents oppose.

The petition's lead ask is a GVR in light of Center for Coalfield Justice v.
Washington County Bd. of Elections, 343 A.3d 1178 (Pa. Sept. 26, 2025), which
petitioner says destroys the panel's premise that voters get no notice or cure
opportunity. Alternatively it seeks plenary review of an acknowledged (panel
op. n.35) 7–4 circuit split over the level of scrutiny for minimally burdensome,
generally applicable election rules under Anderson-Burdick step two.

## Grant-side factors

1. **CVSG** after full briefing — the Court engaged rather than denying or
   summarily GVRing at the June 25 conference.
2. **A federal court of appeals held a state election statute unconstitutional**
   — a classic, near-per-se cert consideration, amplified because the panel
   itself acknowledged it was deepening a circuit split (7–4 per the petition)
   over Anderson-Burdick step-two scrutiny that has festered since Crawford.
3. **Salience and alignment**: election administration in the largest swing
   state; the current Solicitor General is very likely to side with the
   petitioner/RNC position on ballot-integrity enforcement, and post-CVSG grant
   rates are much higher when the SG recommends grant.
4. **The GVR path also counts as a grant here.** Two distinct GVR routes exist:
   (a) the petition's lead ask, GVR in light of Coalfield Justice (Lords
   Landing practice); (b) if the Pennsylvania Supreme Court in Baxter v.
   Philadelphia Bd. of Elections strikes the date requirement under the state
   constitution and moots the federal question, a Munsingwear vacatur — exactly
   what this Court did with the same statute in Ritter v. Migliori, 143 S. Ct.
   297 (2022). Under the schema, gvr joins the granted set.

## Deny-side factors

1. **Vehicle risk / mootness**: Baxter (argued Sept. 10, 2025, still pending as
   of this run) may invalidate the date requirement on independent state
   grounds. If the statute dies anyway, the Court could simply deny rather than
   vacate — though Ritter cuts toward vacatur, and the Commonwealth would press
   for erasing the Third Circuit precedent.
2. The BIO argues the split is manufactured, that Coalfield Justice is far
   narrower than petitioner claims (notice duties arise only where a county
   opts into pre-canvass review and segregation), and that the en banc court
   had Coalfield Justice in hand when it denied rehearing — blunting the GVR
   ask. The Court's choice to CVSG rather than GVR on June 29 suggests it was
   not persuaded to take the cheap GVR route, consistent with the BIO on that
   point.
3. Even post-CVSG, the corpus base rate says denial is the modal outcome
   (71.2% denied in the CVSG bucket), and the SG could recommend denial or a
   hold for Baxter.

## Calibration

Starting anchor: CVSG bucket grant rate 27.1% (corpus statpack; the committed
statpack carries no salience-band table, so the CVSG cut is the closest
available yardstick). I adjust substantially upward for the stacked
grant-positive features rarely all present in the average CVSG case: a state
statute invalidated below, an acknowledged split the panel opted into, a state
petitioner supported by the winning intervenors below, companion petitions, a
CFR before the CVSG, and — decisive for the binary axis — the fact that both
plausible off-ramps (Coalfield Justice GVR, Baxter-mootness Munsingwear vacatur
following Ritter) are grant-coded. Rough scenario arithmetic: if Baxter moots
the case before disposition (~45%), I estimate ~65% of that branch ends in
vacatur/GVR rather than denial or dismissal; if the case stays live (~55%), I
estimate ~55–60% grant (plenary or consolidated with 25-962) given the likely
SG recommendation. That composes to ~0.60–0.65. I set **P(grant incl. GVR) =
0.65**, with the single likeliest disposition **granted** (plenary review,
likely consolidated with No. 25-962), ahead of gvr, then denied.

Timing note: the CVSG issued June 29, 2026, so no disposition is likely before
the SG files (plausibly late 2026), with any plenary merits decision in OT2026
or later. The event predicts disposition, not timing.
