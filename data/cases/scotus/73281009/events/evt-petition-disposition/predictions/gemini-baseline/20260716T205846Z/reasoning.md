The issue is whether Pennsylvania's handwritten date requirement on mail-in ballot return envelopes violates the First and Fourteenth Amendments. The Third Circuit held it did, applying the *Anderson-Burdick* balancing test to strike down the requirement.

The petition was filed by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania, which explicitly asks the Court to grant, vacate, and remand (GVR) the Third Circuit's decision in light of an intervening state supreme court decision, *Center for Coalfield Justice v. Washington County Board of Elections*. The state argues that *Coalfield Justice*, which mandates a notice-and-cure process for undated ballots, fatally undercuts the Third Circuit's burden analysis. Alternatively, the state seeks plenary review to resolve a circuit split over whether *Anderson-Burdick* applies to absentee voting rules. The respondents counter that *Coalfield Justice* does not alter the core constitutional defect, but note the Pennsylvania Supreme Court is currently considering a parallel state constitutional challenge (*Baxter*) which could enjoin the rule on independent state grounds and moot the federal case.

The Supreme Court issued a CVSG (Call for the Views of the Solicitor General) on June 29, 2026. A CVSG in a high-profile election case involving a state attorney general petitioner and a circuit split is a very strong signal of interest. The base rate for a grant following a CVSG is elevated (27.1%), but the specific posture here heavily points toward a GVR. Given the tangled web of intervening state law developments and the pending *Baxter* case in the state supreme court, a CVSG allows the Court to delay action while state courts settle the state-law landscape, or allows the SG to recommend a GVR so the lower court can reconsider its constitutional holding in light of the new state-law notice-and-cure regime. A GVR is the most elegant path for the Court to avoid a messy, highly politicized merits decision immediately preceding the 2026 midterm elections while wiping an arguably flawed Third Circuit precedent off the books. Therefore, I predict a GVR.
