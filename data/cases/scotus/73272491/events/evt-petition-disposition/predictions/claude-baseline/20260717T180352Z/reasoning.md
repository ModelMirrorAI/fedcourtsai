## The legal question

The petition presents a single, clean question: whether *Libretti v. United
States*, 516 U.S. 29 (1995) — which said the Sixth Amendment right to a jury
does not extend to criminal-forfeiture factfinding — should be overruled in
light of *Apprendi v. New Jersey* (2000) and *Southern Union Co. v. United
States* (2012), which require jury findings beyond a reasonable doubt for any
fact that increases the maximum punishment, including criminal fines. Because
forfeitures are "fines" for constitutional purposes (*Bajakajian*), petitioner
argues *Southern Union* logically covers forfeiture, and only the Court can
retire *Libretti* (lower courts uniformly hold they must follow it until then —
*Leahy* (3d Cir. en banc), *Sigillito* (8th Cir.), *Fruchter* (2d Cir.)).

## Facts driving the outcome (from the snapshot and provisioned petition)

Mizrahi, convicted at trial in the S.D.N.Y. of fraud and money-laundering
counts, was ordered to forfeit $4,545,704. The vehicle's most striking feature:
$3,855,704 of that rests on the district judge's *preponderance* finding that
the laundered bulk cash was narcotics proceeds — a predicate never charged, and
one the jury's general verdict (on duplicitous counts covering three alternative
predicates) never found. The Apprendi objection was expressly preserved at
sentencing. The Second Circuit affirmed by unpublished summary order
(Nov. 7, 2025), rehearing denied Jan. 30, 2026.

## Doctrinal trajectory (favors petitioner more than in any prior attempt)

- *Erlinger v. United States* (2024) reaffirmed Apprendi's breadth and pointedly
  catalogued the pre-Apprendi decisions the Court has already overruled — and
  every case *Libretti* cited (*McMillan*, *Cabana*, *Spaziano*) has since been
  overruled or repudiated (*Alleyne*, *Ring*, *Hurst*).
- *Ellingburg v. United States* (No. 24-482, decided Jan. 20, 2026, Kavanaugh,
  J.) — verified via CourtListener — held criminal restitution is criminal
  punishment; the petition quotes it as treating forfeiture under 18 U.S.C.
  § 982(a)(1) as criminal punishment. That freshly strengthens the
  forfeiture-as-fine chain on which the *Southern Union* extension rests.
- Justices Gorsuch and Sotomayor have publicly urged the analogous
  restitution/forfeiture jury-right question (*Hester* (2019) dissent from
  denial; Gorsuch again in *Rimlawi* (2025) and *Burnett* (2026)).

## Why the modal outcome is still denial

- **The Court has denied this exact question at least seven times** (petition's
  own list: *Tedder* and *Braun* (2005), *Leahy* and *Dantone* (2006), *Dantone*
  (2015), *Stevenson* (2017), *Esformes* (2023)), and the restitution analog
  repeatedly since — with only one or two publicly noted votes. Four votes have
  never visibly materialized.
- **No split is possible** — lower courts uniformly follow *Libretti* — so the
  strongest conventional cert criterion is absent; the Court must want to
  revisit its own precedent.
- **Vehicle wrinkles the BIO will press:** the general verdict/duplicity posture
  lets the government argue the $690k bank-fraud slice is jury-supported and the
  dispute is partly case-specific; the decision below is an unpublished summary
  order.
- The CFR guarantees attention, not certiorari; most CFR'd petitions are still
  denied.
