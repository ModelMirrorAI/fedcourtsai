The GEO Group petitions for certiorari following a Ninth Circuit decision holding that Washington State's minimum wage law applies to immigration detainees working at a privately operated federal ICE detention facility. GEO argues this violates the intergovernmental immunity doctrine and is preempted by federal law, asserting a split with three other circuits.

The Supreme Court called for the views of the Solicitor General (CVSG) on May 18, 2026. A CVSG is a strong signal of the Court's interest, carrying a base grant rate of 27.1%. In this case, the likelihood of a grant is significantly higher than the base rate. The Ninth Circuit's decision directly implicates core federal operations and the federal budget, allowing a state to impose its wage laws on a voluntary work program established under federal statutory and contractual authority for immigration detainees. Because the ruling severely disrupts ICE's uniform administration of detention facilities and burdens the federal government (which ultimately bears the cost or operational fallout of its contractors), the Solicitor General is highly likely to recommend granting the petition. 

Given the substantial federalism stakes, the circuit split alleged by the petitioner, and the CVSG indicating the Court's focus on the federal interests involved, this petition is a prime candidate for certiorari. I predict a grant with a probability of 75%.
