# Reasoning: SCOTUS 73377829 (Strickland v. Moritz)

## Legal Question and Standard
The petitioner challenges the constitutionality of the federal judiciary's internal Employment Dispute Resolution (EDR) Plan. The core question is whether the Fifth Amendment’s Due Process and Equal Protection clauses, or the Congressional Accountability Act of 1995, require the judiciary to afford its employees the same substantive and procedural protections available under Title VII of the Civil Rights Act of 1964, rather than adjudicating sexual harassment and discrimination claims through an internal system lacking independent decision-makers. The governing standard for certiorari is whether the petition presents an important question of federal law that has not been, but should be, settled by the Court (Rule 10(c)), or a compelling circuit split (Rule 10(a)).
