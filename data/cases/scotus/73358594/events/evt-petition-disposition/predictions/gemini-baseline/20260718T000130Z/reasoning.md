# Reasoning

## The Legal Question
The petitioner asks whether a federal civilian employee who makes a disclosure premised on both categories enumerated in 5 U.S.C. § 2302(b)(8) is entitled to a Merit Systems Protection Board (MSPB) decision on both categories. Specifically, the petitioner alleges the MSPB and the Federal Circuit only addressed her claim under § 2302(b)(8)(i) (violation of law/rule/regulation) and ignored her claim under § 2302(b)(8)(ii) (gross mismanagement, waste, abuse).

## Governing Standard & Facts
The Supreme Court grants certiorari to resolve circuit splits or to decide important questions of federal law. This case arrives from the Federal Circuit, which has exclusive jurisdiction over most MSPB appeals. Therefore, a circuit split is effectively impossible on this procedural question. The issue presented is characterized entirely as a failure of the lower court and the MSPB to adequately address one of the two prongs of the petitioner's claim. The Supreme Court rarely takes cases solely for error correction, especially fact-bound procedural errors by the MSPB. 

Furthermore, the Solicitor General (representing the respondent Department of Agriculture) waived the right to respond on June 8, 2026. While the Court can call for a response (CVSG or a direct order) if it is interested in a case, a waiver signals that the government views the petition as unworthy of the Court's resources. The petition was distributed for the "long conference" of September 28, 2026. A petition without a response is almost never granted outright. 

## Base Rates & Priors
The base rate for modern discretionary-cert petitions from the Federal Circuit is heavily skewed towards denial (93.6% denied). The overall grant rate for cases with 0 relists is only 0.8%. 

## Prediction
Given the lack of a circuit split, the error-correction nature of the petition, the respondent's waiver, and the strong base-rate priors, this petition has virtually no chance of being granted. 

Predicted Disposition: Denied
