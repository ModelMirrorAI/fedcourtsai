# Texas Top Cop Shop, Inc. v. Blanche — No. 25-1290 (petition disposition)

## The question

Petition for a writ of certiorari **before judgment** (filed May 2026 from the
Fifth Circuit, No. 24-40792, which has not yet decided the appeal), asking the
Court to resolve the facial constitutionality of the Corporate Transparency
Act's beneficial-ownership reporting mandate — chiefly whether Congress's
commerce and necessary-and-proper powers reach a reporting requirement imposed
on entities solely because they are formed under state law. Petitioners are
represented by the Center for Individual Rights; respondent is the United
States (Acting Attorney General Blanche), with the Solicitor General as counsel
of record.

## Posture and record facts driving the call (snapshot of 2026-07-17)

- **Cert before judgment.** The Fifth Circuit appeal is still pending
  (CourtListener shows No. 24-40792 not terminated), so Rule 11's
  "imperative public importance" standard applies — a posture the Court
  grants rarely and, in recent practice, almost always when the government
  itself seeks or acquiesces in immediate review. Here the government did
  neither.
- **The government waived its response** (May 26, 2026), and as of the
  snapshot the Court has **not called for a response**. The Court's settled
  practice is to CFR before granting any petition on which the respondent
  waived; no CFR on file means a grant cannot be imminent, and the modal
  outcome from this position is denial.
- **Distributed June 2 for the June 18 conference, then "Rescheduled" on
  June 5** with no re-distribution before the Term ended. The natural reading
  is that the Court is aligning this petition with its companion, **No.
  25-1201, National Small Business United v. Bessent** — the Eleventh
  Circuit case in which that court **upheld the CTA on the merits (Dec. 16,
  2025)**, and whose cert petition (filed April 15, 2026, on an extension
  granted by Justice Thomas) came ready later than this one. Both petitions
  now ride to the fall long conference. Rescheduling-to-pair signals real
  consideration of the CTA question, but the final-judgment NSBU petition is
  the natural lead vehicle; this interlocutory petition is the tag-along.
- **Unusually strong amicus support for a cert-stage petition**: West
  Virginia and 24 other states, the Buckeye Institute, Advancing American
  Freedom, and a joint America's Future / Gun Owners of America brief filed
  across both 25-1201 and 25-1290. Paid petition, sophisticated counsel.
- **Prior SCOTUS engagement in this same litigation**: the Court stayed the
  district court's nationwide injunction in January 2025 (McHenry v. Texas
  Top Cop Shop), so the case is familiar and salient to the Justices. But the
  stay itself suggests a Court majority not persuaded the challengers will
  prevail, and the separate question that then interested some Justices —
  the propriety of universal injunctions — has since been resolved elsewhere
  (Trump v. CASA, June 2025), removing that hook.
- **Deflated practical stakes**: FinCEN's March 2025 interim final rule
  exempted domestic reporting companies from the CTA's reporting
  requirements, which both mutes the urgency Rule 11 demands and gives the
  government a vehicle/mootness argument against review.
- **No circuit split.** The only court of appeals to decide the question
  (the Eleventh) *upheld* the statute; the Fifth Circuit has been sitting on
  its appeal since argument. The Court rarely grants to review a decision
  the petition's side lost below absent a split or SG support — and it
  especially rarely grants *before judgment* to create the vehicle itself.

## Weighing

Base rates (committed statpack, modern discretionary-cert slice): resolved
petitions run ~95% denied / ~3% granted; Term 2025 estimated grant rate 2.5%;
fifth-circuit-originating petitions ~4.8% granted; petitions never relisted
grant at ~0.8%, one relist ~7.6%, two ~33.6%. This petition has not yet been
considered at any conference (one pre-conference reschedule), so the relist
signal is not yet in play.

Upward adjustments from the base rate: the 25-state amicus bloc, the paired
final-judgment vehicle raising the identical question, prior SCOTUS
involvement, the deliberate reschedule-to-pair rather than a routine denial at
the June 18 conference, and a Court receptive in recent Terms to
cert-before-judgment in structurally important cases. Downward: no CFR despite
a government waiver (near-dispositive against a near-term grant), interlocutory
posture with the Fifth Circuit ruling still expected, no split, government
opposition/waiver, and the FinCEN rule draining the practical urgency.

The realistic grant path is: the Court CFRs over the summer, considers both
petitions at the long conference, grants **NSBU (25-1201)** as the lead
vehicle, and — if it wants the Fifth Circuit record or the broader petitioner
class — consolidates this petition with it. I put roughly 25–30% on the Court
taking the CTA question this cycle at all (no split, statute upheld below,
likely SG opposition, mootness overhang), and roughly 40–50% on this petition
being included in any such grant rather than denied or held alongside NSBU.
That compounds to ~10–14%. If the Court passes on the question, this petition
is denied outright, likely without recorded dissent; if the Fifth Circuit
finally decides while the petitions pend, the cert-before-judgment rationale
evaporates and denial follows almost automatically (with a fresh
post-judgment petition to come later — outside this event).

GVR is not available in this posture (there is no judgment below to vacate),
and nothing suggests dismissal or withdrawal.

## Call

- **predicted_disposition: denied**, granted = 0
- **probability (P(any grant)): 0.12** — well above the ~3% modern paid-cert
  base rate on the strength of the pairing, the state amici, and the
  reschedule signal, but held under 15% by the no-CFR-after-waiver posture,
  the interlocutory vehicle problem, the absence of a split, and the
  companion case's superiority as a vehicle.
- **confidence: 0.6**; **big_case_score: 0.78** — a merits decision on the
  CTA's facial constitutionality would be a landmark enumerated-powers case
  affecting tens of millions of state-formed entities, independent of how
  likely this particular petition is to be the vehicle.
