## Certiorari assessment

The governing federal rules are settled at a high level. Under *Terry v. Ohio*, a stop requires particularized reasonable suspicion, and a frisk additionally requires reasonable suspicion that the person is armed and dangerous. *Florida v. J.L.* makes an uncorroborated anonymous tip generally insufficient, while the totality of corroborating observations can matter. *Scott v. Harris* addresses treatment of video evidence that blatantly contradicts a party's account. Certiorari ordinarily requires more than an asserted misapplication of those standards: a developed conflict among lower courts, an important unsettled federal question, or an unusually clear and consequential departure from precedent.

This petition is a poor vehicle for that review.
