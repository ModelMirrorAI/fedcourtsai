This is a pro se petition by Christopher Veto seeking review of a Ninth Circuit decision affirming summary judgment for Boeing on his employment retaliation claims. The petitioner alleges that he smelled marijuana at work and was subsequently retaliated against for reporting it. He advances sweeping constitutional arguments, contending that state marijuana legalization laws violate the Supremacy and Commerce Clauses, and asks the Court to issue a nationwide injunction against Boeing, seize Boeing aircraft, disband the FAA's ODA, and force the breakup of Boeing into separate corporations.

These claims are factually and legally frivolous. The Supreme Court is a court of discretionary review that resolves circuit splits on important federal questions. It does not engage in fact-bound error correction of summary judgment rulings in routine employment retaliation cases, nor does it entertain extravagant remedies like the dissolution of major corporations or the seizure of aircraft. The constitutional challenges to state marijuana laws are raised out of context in a private employment dispute. The petition lacks any compelling reason for the Court to grant certiorari under Supreme Court Rule 10, and aligns with the typical profile of a pro se petition that will be summarily denied.

According to the corpus base rates, the cert grant rate for paid petitions in recent terms is generally low (~2.5%), and the claims made here place the likelihood of a grant near zero. Therefore, the most probable outcome is a denial.
