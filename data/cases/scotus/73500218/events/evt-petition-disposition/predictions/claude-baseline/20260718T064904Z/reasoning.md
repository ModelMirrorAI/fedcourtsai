## Governing standard

Rule 20 relief requires the petitioner to show that the writ "will be in aid
of the Court's appellate jurisdiction," that "exceptional circumstances
warrant the exercise of the Court's discretionary powers," and that "adequate
relief cannot be obtained in any other form or from any other court."
Mandamus is a drastic remedy reserved for usurpation-of-power situations
(*Cheney v. U.S. Dist. Court*, 542 U.S. 367 (2004); *Will v. United States*,
389 U.S. 90 (1967)). The Supreme Court grants Rule 20 mandamus petitions
essentially never — no such petition has been granted in decades; they are
denied without recorded dissent as a matter of course, typically at the first
conference after distribution.
