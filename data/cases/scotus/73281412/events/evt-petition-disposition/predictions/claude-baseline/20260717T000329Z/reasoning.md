# Alexander v. Philip R. Taft Psy D and Associates, P.L.L.C. (No. 25-1128) — cert prediction

**Prediction: deny, P(grant incl. GVR) = 0.09.**

## The case

Ronnie Alexander, a pretrial detainee at the Henderson County (TX) jail, told an
officer he was suicidal — he says falsely, to escape cellmates who were
threatening him — and spent five-plus days in a bare "violent cell" with no
bunk, toilet, running water, clothing (beyond a suicide blanket), or hygiene
access, under 24-hour lighting, allegedly with taunts and threats from guards.
His § 1983 conditions-of-confinement and inadequate-mental-health-care claims
were dismissed at 12(b)(6); a divided Fifth Circuit panel affirmed in a
published opinion (143 F.4th 569, substituted on rehearing denial at 165 F.4th
309), Judge Dennis dissenting both times, with an expanded dissent on rehearing.

The petition (filed March 23, 2026, paid, counseled by Texas practitioners)
presents three questions: (1) whether jail mental-health providers' Estelle
obligations are limited to "protection from violence and suicide"; (2) whether
Bell v. Wolfish's excessiveness/proportionality prong survives in the Fifth
Circuit after the panel held that "Bell looks only for a reasonable
relationship, not narrow tailoring"; and (3) whether a court may categorically
refuse to draw state-of-mind inferences as "speculative" at the pleading stage,
notwithstanding NRA v. Vullo.

## Weighing merits and vehicle

**For grant.** The CFR is real and the underlying opinion is published and
precedential, so the Fifth Circuit's holdings will govern a very large detainee
population. The petition alleges two plausible tensions: virtually every
circuit treats serious mental-health needs as within Estelle, and several
circuits (CA2, CA3, CA4, CA6, CA7, CA8, CA10) apply Bell's excessiveness prong
in ways hard to square with the panel's "reasonable relationship, not narrow
tailoring" formulation. Judge Dennis's dissent ("punishment of a pretrial
detainee disguised as suicide watch") gives the petition a ready-made
narrative, and the facts echo Taylor v. Riojas, where this Court summarily
reversed the Fifth Circuit over comparably squalid cell conditions. A
summary-reversal or GVR path exists in principle, which is part of why my
probability sits above the paid base rate.

**Against grant.** The vehicle is messy. Alexander *admits* he told jail staff
he was suicidal and never retracted it; the BIO argues forcefully that every
challenged condition is a standard, TCJS-approved suicide-prevention measure,
and that the jail faced a damned-if-you-do dilemma the panel expressly relied
on. That framing converts the case from "circuit adopts lawless rule" into
"circuit applied Bell to unsympathetic facts" — the BIO plausibly recharacterizes
the "protection from violence or suicide" sentence as a quotation in a
Deshaney/Hare doctrinal passage rather than a new ceiling on Estelle duties.
Splits that depend on how one reads a contested sentence of the opinion below
are the kind the Court usually lets percolate. The posture (12(b)(6), qualified
immunity issues unresolved below) and QP3, which is naked error-correction of
inference-drawing, further weaken it. There are no amici flagging the case, and
counsel are not repeat Supreme Court practitioners. Taylor v. Riojas was also a
different shape: an egregious-facts qualified-immunity summary reversal, not a
doctrinal cert grant, and there the detainee had not manufactured the
triggering condition.

## Calibration

Start from the paid-petition anchor (~5.5%). The CFR conditional uplift
(roughly 2–3x in the historical literature and consistent with the corpus
relist/CVSG-style signal structure) pushes upward; the vehicle problems, the
absence of amici, and the error-correction flavor pull back down. I land at
**0.09** for P(any grant, GVR included), with the modal outcome a **denial** at
the long conference or after one or two relists — possibly with a dissent from
denial by Justices Sotomayor and/or Jackson given the Taylor v. Riojas
resonance. Within the grant mass I'd apportion roughly 6% plenary/summary
grant and 3% GVR-like disposition; denied remains the clear modal label.
