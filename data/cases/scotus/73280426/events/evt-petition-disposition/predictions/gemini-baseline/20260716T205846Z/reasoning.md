# Reasoning for Prediction

## The Legal Question and Standard
The petition presents two questions arising from a Voting Rights Act (VRA) Section 2 challenge to Washington state's legislative district 15. The district court found a VRA violation and imposed a remedial map. The petitioners are intervenors who seek to challenge both the underlying VRA liability finding and the remedial map (as an unconstitutional racial gerrymander). The Ninth Circuit held the intervenors lacked Article III standing to appeal the liability determination or challenge the remedial map for vote dilution, and further held that strict scrutiny did not apply to the remedial map.

The questions are:
1. Whether a voter moved to a new district and a legislator whose district is reconfigured by a remedial map have standing to challenge that map and the underlying VRA liability determination.
2. Whether a race-conscious map drawn to remedy racial vote dilution is subject to strict scrutiny under the Equal Protection Clause.

## Key Facts and Record Evidence
- **Postures and Parties:** The State of Washington (Secretary of State) was the original defendant but accepted the district court's ruling and the remedial map. The petitioners are intervenors.
- **Waivers and CFR:** Both sets of respondents (the original plaintiffs and the State) initially waived their right to respond. The Supreme Court then requested a response (Call for Response, or CFR).
- **Brief in Opposition:** The respondents strongly argue that this case is a poor vehicle due to threshold jurisdictional defects (lack of intervenor standing), lack of a circuit split, and forfeiture of the racial gerrymandering claim below. They also argue the decision comports with recent Supreme Court precedent (*Louisiana v. Callais*).
- **Current Status:** The petition is fully briefed and distributed for the September 28, 2026 Long Conference. It has 0 relists.

## Reasoning for Probability and Disposition
While a Call for Response is a positive signal that at least one Justice was interested in the petition (raising the base grant rate from ~0.8% to around 5-8%), several case-specific factors strongly point to a denial:
1. **No Circuit Split:** The petition does not allege a deep or mature circuit split on the questions presented.
2. **Vehicle Problems:** The respondents forcefully argue that the intervenors lack standing and forfeited their key racial gerrymandering arguments below. The Court is generally reluctant to grant certiorari when threshold jurisdictional and procedural issues could prevent reaching the merits.
3. **State Opposition:** Unlike many election cases where the state is appealing the invalidation of its legislative maps, here the State of Washington is a respondent opposing certiorari, having accepted the remedial map. This makes the intervenors' position significantly weaker as a candidate for Supreme Court review.
4. **Relist Count:** The petition is distributed for the Long Conference and has no relists. Petitions granted out of the Long Conference are relatively rare compared to the volume of denials, and the lack of state support further depresses the odds.

Given the vehicle issues, lack of a split, and the state's posture, the most likely outcome is that the petition will be denied.

**Predicted Disposition:** denied
**Probability of Grant:** 0.04
**Big Case Score:** 0.40 (The case involves VRA Section 2 and racial gerrymandering, which are highly salient topics, but the specific posture of intervenors appealing an accepted map lowers its national significance).
