# Reasoning

## Legal Question and Standard
The underlying petition asks whether plaintiffs can toll the four-year Clayton Act statute of limitations (15 U.S.C. § 15b) by alleging an unwritten conspiratorial agreement, creating a circuit split on the pleading standards for fraudulent concealment in antitrust cases. 
However, the immediate question facing the Court is procedural. Following a CVSG order (which indicates high Court interest in the circuit split), the respondent voluntarily dismissed her claims against the petitioners (General Dynamics), while pursuing settlements with the other defendants. Petitioners have requested the petition be held in abeyance pending district court approval of those settlements, after which they will seek vacatur of the Fourth Circuit's judgment under *United States v. Munsingwear, Inc.*, 340 U.S. 36 (1950).

## Facts and Analysis
1. **CVSG and Merits Interest:** The Court invited the Solicitor General to file a brief expressing the views of the United States on January 12, 2026. A CVSG is a strong signal that the Court is considering a grant, recognizing the importance of the circuit split regarding antitrust pleading standards.
2. **Voluntary Dismissal and Mootness:** On May 18, 2026, petitioners informed the Court that respondent had voluntarily dismissed her claims against them, and that the case would become moot once class settlements with other defendants are finalized. 
3. **Munsingwear Vacatur:** While mootness caused by settlement generally does not justify vacatur (*U.S. Bancorp Mortgage Co. v. Bonner Mall Partnership*, 513 U.S. 18 (1994)), mootness caused by the unilateral action of the prevailing party below (i.e., respondent voluntarily dismissing claims against petitioners to avoid Supreme Court review) is the paradigmatic situation where *Munsingwear* vacatur is equitable and appropriate. 

## Prediction
Because respondent unilaterally dismissed the claims against General Dynamics, likely to frustrate Supreme Court review of an adverse circuit precedent, General Dynamics has a very strong equitable claim to a *Munsingwear* vacatur. A GVR (Grant, Vacate, and Remand) technically disposes of the petition for certiorari by granting it. Therefore, I predict the Court will hold the petition in abeyance as requested, and ultimately issue a GVR (granting the petition) with instructions to dismiss the case as moot.
