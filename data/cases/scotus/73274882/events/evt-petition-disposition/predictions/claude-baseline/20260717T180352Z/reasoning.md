# Duncan v. Bonta, No. 25-198 — cert disposition prediction

**Prediction: granted, P(any grant incl. GVR) = 0.62.** Forward cell; the
petition is genuinely pending as of the 2026-07-17 snapshot.

## The legal question

Petitioners (Virginia Duncan et al. and the California Rifle & Pistol
Association, represented by Clement & Murphy) challenge California Penal Code
§ 32310, which bans possession of ammunition-feeding devices holding more than
ten rounds — including retrospectively, requiring dispossession of magazines
lawfully acquired decades ago. QP1: whether a ban on "exceedingly common"
feeding devices violates the Second Amendment under Heller/Bruen's
common-use rule. QP2: whether uncompensated compelled dispossession violates
the Takings Clause. The en banc Ninth Circuit (7–4, March 20, 2025) upheld the
ban after this Court's 2022 GVR in light of Bruen, holding that 10+-round
magazines are "accoutrements," not "Arms," and alternatively that the ban fits
a historical tradition of regulating especially dangerous weapons.

## The governing standard (what drives a cert grant)

Certiorari turns on a square conflict on an important federal question, vehicle
quality, and four votes. The BIO's core arguments: the Court denied three
materially identical LCM petitions in the last two years (Harrel 2024; Ocean
State Tactical and Hanson, June 2025); there is no genuine circuit conflict
(every federal circuit to decide — 1st, 2d, 4th, 7th, 9th, 10th, D.C. — has
upheld LCM restrictions at some posture); and percolation continues (3d Cir.
en banc ANJRPC and 7th Cir. Barnett, both final-judgment appeals, argued
fall 2025 and still pending).

## Case-specific facts that drive the forecast

1. **A record-breaking relist marathon.** Docketed Aug 19, 2025; fully briefed
   Nov 5, 2025; distributed for ~23 consecutive conferences from 11/21/2025
   through the end-of-Term mop-up conference of 6/29/2026 — past the modern
   relist record (~22, Ryan v. Hurles). This is not passive neglect: the Court
   repeatedly, affirmatively carried it.
2. **It survived the Term-end cleanup.** Corpus rows show grants from the
   6/29/2026 conference issuing 6/30 (e.g., the Monsanto petitions), and the
   docket refreshed 7/14/2026 shows no order in Duncan — no grant, no denial,
   no GVR. When the Court cleaned house after deciding Wolford v. Lopez
   (6/25/2026), it chose not to dispose of Duncan, carrying it over the summer
   recess. In Snope (the closest analogue), denial came *before* Term end
   after ~14 relists. Choosing not to deny when denial was costless is the
   single most probative new fact.
3. **The vehicle objection from the 2025 denials is gone.** This is a final
   judgment after full trial-court record development and en banc merits
   review — precisely what Justice Kavanaugh's Snope statement (145 S. Ct.
   1534) said the Court would benefit from, while predicting the Court
   "presumably should and will" address the arms-ban question "soon."
4. **Benson.** On March 5, 2026 a D.C. Court of Appeals panel (Benson v.
   United States) struck down D.C.'s identical 10-round cap, creating the
   first appellate holding invalidating an LCM ban; petitioners filed a
   supplemental brief claiming a square split (3/11/2026). On April 22, 2026
   the full D.C. Court of Appeals granted rehearing en banc and vacated the
   panel opinion — reported to the Court in respondent's 4/23 letter — so the
   clean new split evaporated, but the near-split shows the alignment is
   cracking.
5. **The 2A docket context.** The Court decided Wolford v. Lopez 6–3 for the
   gun-rights side on June 25, 2026 (Alito, J.), and United States v. Hemani
   this Term; the Third Circuit en banc called for post-Wolford supplemental
   briefs (due July 10, 2026) in the New Jersey AWB/LCM case. Companion
   arms-ban petitions — Gator's Custom Guns v. Washington (25-153), NAGR v.
   Lamont (25-421), Grant v. Higgins (25-566) — are being warehoused alongside
   Duncan.

## Base rates and adjustment

From the committed statpack (live/historical slice): modern discretionary-cert
grant rate ≈ 2.5–3% per Term; ca9-originating petitions in the salience-selected
slice grant at ~16.5%. The relist cut is the operative signal: 3+ relists →
granted 21.8% / denied 76.5%. Duncan sits at the extreme tail of that bucket
(~23 distributions), where the published-bucket rate under-describes the case;
the statpack carries no salience-band table to refine further.

Ultra-long relists historically resolve two ways: denial with published
dissent(s) (Snope, Ocean State, Apache Stronghold, Harrel — and the Court has
denied this precise LCM question four times since 2024), or a deliberated
blockbuster grant (Dobbs was relisted ~a dozen times across most of a Term
before grant; Fulton and 303 Creative similarly). What separates Duncan from
the denial cluster: it is the first *final-judgment* vehicle; Kavanaugh's
Snope statement all but promised review within "the next Term or two" (OT2026
is that Term); the Wolford majority shows a working 6–3 Second Amendment
majority; and the Court declined to deny at the mop-up conference when the
Snope playbook was fully available. What keeps the probability well below
certainty: the Court's revealed preference on this exact issue is four straight
denials; the Third Circuit and Seventh Circuit final-judgment decisions plus
the Benson en banc are still pending, giving a percolation-minded fifth-or-
fourth justice a reason to deny again (with dissents drafted over the summer,
released on an early-OT2026 order list); and 8 months of relisting without a
grant suggests the fourth vote has been genuinely hesitant.

Weighing these: P(grant, including a small ~0.05 GVR branch) ≈ 0.62;
P(denied) ≈ 0.37; P(dismissed/other) ≈ 0.01. Modal disposition: plenary grant
at or shortly after the September 2026 long conference, argued OT2026.

## Takings QP

QP2 is a makeweight at the cert stage (no conflict; every court to consider a
like claim has rejected it), but it adds optionality: a grant could take both
questions or be limited to QP1. It does not materially move P(grant).
