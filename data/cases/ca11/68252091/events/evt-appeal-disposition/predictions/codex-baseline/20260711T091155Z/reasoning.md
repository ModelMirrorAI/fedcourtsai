## Governing standard

Mandamus under the All Writs Act is a drastic, extraordinary remedy, not a substitute for an ordinary appeal. A petitioner generally must show that no other adequate means exist to obtain relief, that the right to issuance of the writ is clear and indisputable, and that issuing the writ is appropriate in the circumstances. In supervising a district court, the court of appeals ordinarily reserves mandamus for a clear usurpation of power, a failure to exercise jurisdiction, or comparably exceptional error. An issue that can be addressed through ordinary appellate review usually defeats mandamus relief.
