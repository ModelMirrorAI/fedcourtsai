Mandamus under the All Writs Act, 28 U.S.C. § 1651(a), is a drastic, extraordinary remedy. The petitioner generally must show that no other adequate means exist to obtain relief, that the right to issuance is clear and indisputable, and that issuance is appropriate in the circumstances. See *Cheney v. U.S. District Court*, 542 U.S. 367, 380–81 (2004). It is not a substitute for an ordinary appeal and ordinarily requires much more than an arguable lower-court error.
