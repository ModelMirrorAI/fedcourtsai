# Prediction: denied

I assign a 4% probability that the Eleventh Circuit grants Carl Puckett's motion to reconsider the March 15, 2024 panel order, and predict denial.

## Legal question and standard

The March 15 order concluded that the notice of appeal was not yet effective because the district court had not resolved the Pucketts' construed motions for reconsideration. Citing Federal Rule of Appellate Procedure 4(a)(4)(B)(i), the panel directed the district court to decide those motions before the appeal proceeded. The question is whether the panel should revisit that procedural ruling.

Reconsideration is an exceptional remedy directed to a material legal or factual error, an intervening change in law, or materially new information. It is not ordinarily a vehicle to repeat arguments the panel has already considered. Rule 4(a)(4)(B)(i) expressly postpones the effectiveness of a notice of appeal filed before disposition of a qualifying post-judgment motion, so the panel's stated approach tracks the rule's text.
