## The legal question and governing standard

Whether the Eleventh Circuit will consolidate two pending appeals. Under
Fed. R. App. P. 3(b)(2) and circuit practice, a court of appeals may
consolidate appeals on its own motion or a party's when they involve common
parties and overlapping questions. Consolidation is an administrative,
efficiency-driven ruling, not a merits ruling; the bar is low and such motions
are granted at high rates — especially where, as here, both appeals arise from
the **same underlying district-court case**.

  4(a)(4)(B)(i) pending the district court's rulings on construed

- The Eleventh Circuit sometimes achieves the same efficiency by treating
