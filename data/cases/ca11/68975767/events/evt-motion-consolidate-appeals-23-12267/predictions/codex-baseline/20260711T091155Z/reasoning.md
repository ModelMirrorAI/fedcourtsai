## Legal question and standard

The question is whether the court will consolidate two appeals identified by the movant. Federal Rule of Appellate Procedure 3(b)(2) permits a court of appeals to join or consolidate separate appeals. The decision is discretionary and ordinarily turns on whether the appeals substantially overlap in parties, record, and issues, and whether consolidation would improve efficiency without creating procedural confusion or prejudice.
