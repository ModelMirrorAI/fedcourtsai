## Question and governing standards

The event is keyed to Puckett's amended notice of appeal. The snapshot indicates that the appeal concerns orders dismissing his amended counterclaim and denying his motion for summary judgment and injunctive relief as moot after Ain Jeem voluntarily dismissed its claim. It also presents a threshold timing issue under Federal Rule of Appellate Procedure 4(a)(4)(B)(i): a notice of appeal filed while qualifying reconsideration motions remain pending is suspended and becomes effective only when those motions are resolved.

On the merits, dismissal of a pleading ordinarily receives de novo review, while denial of reconsideration is reviewed for abuse of discretion. Reconsideration is an extraordinary remedy; absent new evidence or a change in controlling law, the movant must show clear error or manifest injustice. The appellant must also identify a live controversy and reversible error rather than merely repeat arguments rejected below.
